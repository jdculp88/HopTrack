## Our 8 Key Differentiators

### 1. Design
Untappd looks like 2012. BeerAdvocate looks like 2008. HopTrack is the first beer app that belongs next to Spotify and Airbnb aesthetically. Editorial typography, warm color system, spring animations, Wrapped-style shareability. Design is a legitimate moat with our target demographic (25–40 year old urban craft drinkers).

### 2. Independence — Not Owned by Big Beer
RateBeer's acquisition by AB InBev in 2017 permanently damaged trust with the craft community. We lead with independence on our about page, marketing, and onboarding. This is a values statement, not just positioning.

### 3. Two-Sided Platform
No competitor does both consumer and brewery tools well. Untappd neglects the brewery side (their tools are weak and overpriced). We build for both and create a flywheel: more consumers → more value for breweries → more breweries enroll → better data and loyalty perks for consumers.

### 4. Brewery Loyalty System
Paper punch cards are still the norm at 90%+ of craft breweries. We replace them with digital stamp cards, QR redemption, and brewery-controlled offer rules. High perceived value, direct ROI for breweries, sticky for consumers.

### 5. TV Display / Video Board
Real-time taproom display showing live check-ins, leaderboards, beer stats, and achievement unlocks. Runs on any TV via Chrome kiosk mode. Self-reinforcing: patrons see their name on screen → share on social → non-users download the app. Completely novel — no competitor has this.

### 6. Wrapped-Style Recaps
Weekly, monthly, and yearly shareable recap cards. Year in Beer goes viral the same way Spotify Wrapped does. Every shared recap is free marketing to every non-user in that person's network. Untappd has no equivalent.

### 7. Location Intelligence & Brewery Suggestions
Aggregate anonymized check-in data by geography to surface actionable insights for breweries: trending styles in their area, gaps in their tap list, seasonal timing recommendations. "Kolsch is the #1 style checked in within 5 miles — you don't carry one." Premium tier feature with no competitive equivalent.

### 8. Data Moat
The longer we operate, the more valuable our data becomes. Network effects mean insights and recommendations improve as we scale. Eventually a data product for distributors, ingredient suppliers, and hospitality groups.

## Target Customer Profiles

### Consumer
- Age 25–42, urban or suburban
- Drinks craft beer at least 2x/month
- Already uses Untappd OR is a lapsed user frustrated with stale UX
- Values aesthetics, social sharing, and being recognized as a regular
- Motivated by: achievements, leaderboards, Year in Beer recap, loyalty rewards

### Brewery (B2B)
- Independent craft brewery, 500–50,000 BBL/year
- 1–3 taproom locations
- Currently using: paper punch cards, Untappd Business, or nothing
- Pain points: no customer data, no digital loyalty, no way to know what's selling and why
- Budget: $50–300/month for tools that demonstrably increase taproom revenue

## Go-To-Market Summary (Taylor — Sales)

**Phase 1 — Build the consumer base**
Launch free consumer app. Acquire first 10K users organically via App Store, craft beer subreddits, Instagram, and word of mouth. Focus on cities with dense craft brewery scenes: Portland, Denver, Asheville, Chicago, San Diego, Austin, Brooklyn.

**Phase 2 — Convert breweries**
Once 10K+ users are in 3–5 metro areas, approach local breweries with check-in data in hand. "Here are 847 HopTrack users who've checked in within 2 miles of your brewery and don't have a loyalty card with you yet." That's the cold call closer.

**Phase 3 — Scale B2B**
Trade show presence (GABF, CBC). Sales team targeting brewery associations. Referral program: brewery recommends HopTrack to neighboring breweries. PR around Year in Beer viral moment.
