## 1. Name Validation

### "HopTrack" — Recommended: KEEP ✅

**Why it works:**
- **Hops** = the defining ingredient of beer, immediately signals the category
- **Track** = three meanings that all serve us: track your check-ins, track your journey, track record
- Two syllables, easy to say, easy to remember, easy to spell
- No trademark conflicts found in the app space (Untappd, Taphunter, RateBeer — all clear)
- Strong .com availability potential (hoptrack.app, trackeverypour.com as alternatives)
- Works as a verb: "I HopTracked it" / "Did you HopTrack that?"

**Considered alternatives (rejected):**
| Name | Why rejected |
|------|-------------|
| BrewLog | Generic, no personality |
| TapRoot | Good but sounds like a tech company |
| PintTrack | Too literal |
| CraftPath | Forgettable |
| Malty | Too cute, no tracking implication |

**Verdict:** HopTrack is the name. Moving forward with confidence.

## 2. Logo Concepts

### Concept A — "The Hop Pin" ⭐ (Recommended)
A hop cone (the actual plant) morphed into a location pin. The bottom point of the pin is the stem of the hop. Gold fill on dark circle background. Clean, instantly reads as both "beer" and "location/tracking."

**Usage:** App icon, favicon, letterhead, merchandise.
**Variations:** Full color (gold on dark), monochrome (white on dark), reversed (dark on gold)

### Concept B — "The Trail"
A stylized hop leaf with a path/trail curving through it — referencing both the ingredient and the journey. More sophisticated, editorial. Better for marketing materials than app icon (too detailed at small sizes).

### Concept C — "The Glass"
A pint glass silhouette with a hop cone as the foam head. Classic, immediately readable. Risk: similar to competitors (BrewDog, many others use glass silhouettes). Less differentiated.

**Recommendation:** Concept A for the primary mark. Concept B for marketing illustrations. Retire Concept C.

## 4. Brand Voice

### Personality Pillars
1. **Knowledgeable but never snobby** — We know our IPAs from our DIPAs but we celebrate the person ordering a Coors Light too. Beer should be joyful.
2. **Warm and inviting** — Like a good taproom. You walk in and feel welcome immediately, whether it's your first craft beer or your five hundredth.
3. **Playful with purpose** — We make jokes but we're building a real product. Think Duolingo meets Vivino — gamified but grounded.
4. **Independently minded** — We root for the little guys. Small breweries, independent owners, people who care about quality over quantity.

### Voice in Practice
| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Empty state | "No check-ins yet — your story starts with the first pour 🍺" |
| Achievement unlock | "You've earned it. Cheers, explorer." |
| Push notification | "Your crew is at Trillium right now. Join them?" |
| Error state | "Something went sideways. Probably not the beer's fault." |
| Brewery onboarding | "Welcome to HopTrack for Breweries. Let's meet your regulars." |
| Loading | "Pouring your feed..." |

### What we are NOT:
- 🚫 Bro-y or aggressive ("CRUSH that IPA!!!")
- 🚫 Corporate or sterile ("Thank you for using our platform.")
- 🚫 Pretentious ("The terroir of this double dry-hopped...")
- 🚫 Desperate ("Like and share for a chance to win!")

## 5. Visual Identity

### Typography (already in codebase — confirm as brand standards)
- **Playfair Display** — headlines, brewery names, editorial moments. Signals quality and tradition.
- **DM Sans** — body copy, UI labels, everything else. Clean, modern, friendly.
- **JetBrains Mono** — numbers, stats, level indicators, ratings. Technical precision.

### Color System (already established — confirm as brand standards)
| Role | Dark Mode | Light Mode |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Background | `#0F0E0C` Espresso | `#FAF7F2` Cream |
| Surface | `#1C1A16` Dark Mahogany | `#FFFFFF` White |
| Gold Accent | `#D4A843` Amber Gold | `#C49A35` Deep Gold |
| Amber Accent | `#E8841A` Warm Amber | `#D4730E` |

**Named palette for marketing use:**
- Espresso, Mahogany, Grain, Parchment, Cream
- Amber Gold, Warm Amber, Harvest
- Slate, Ash, Smoke

### Photography Style
- Warm, natural light
- Real taprooms, real people, real pints (no stock photo staging)
- Shallow depth of field on beer close-ups
- Hands holding glasses — personal, not product-shoot clinical
- Golden hour / warm tungsten lighting preferred
