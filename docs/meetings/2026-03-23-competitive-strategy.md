# HopTrack Meeting — Competitive Differentiation & Strategy
**Date:** 2026-03-23

## Key Insights from Drew (Industry Expert)
- Untappd completely neglects the brewery side — charges $200–400/mo for weak tools
- 9,500+ craft breweries in the US, most are 2–10 person operations starved for customer data
- RateBeer's AB InBev acquisition in 2017 destroyed trust — 3M disillusioned users looking for alternative
- TV display feature alone is worth $149/mo to a taproom owner — "I would have paid for it in a heartbeat"
- The TV display is a self-reinforcing growth loop: patrons see their name → share on social → non-users download

## Key Insights from Taylor (Sales)
- Two-sided market opportunity: breweries want customers, customers want discovery and recognition
- Untappd is the MySpace of beer apps — people use it out of habit, not because it's good
- Lead consumer acquisition with the social feed: "the app you open Monday morning to see what your friends drank"
- Cold call closer: show brewery the check-in data from their neighborhood before you call
- TV display is the demo closer — always show it live

## Decisions Made
- Positioning: *"The app for craft drinkers. The tool craft breweries actually need."*
- Lead with independence / not-owned-by-Big-Beer in all consumer-facing messaging
- Four brewery pricing tiers: Free / Tap ($49) / Barrel ($149) / Cask ($299)
- Phase 1 GTM: 10 launch cities with dense craft beer scenes
- Priority trade shows: CBC (spring) and GABF (October/Denver)
- Drew available as industry reference and brewery network introductions
