# HopTrack — Pitch Deck Outline
**Owner:** Taylor · **Format:** 10–12 slides · **Updated:** 2026-03-27

---

This is the slide structure for the HopTrack brewery pitch deck. Keep it tight. A brewery owner has 15 minutes between pours, not 45. Every slide should be one idea, one image, one punch.

> **Design note (Jamie):** Dark background (#0F0E0C), gold accents (#D4A843), Playfair Display headlines, DM Sans body. Same system as the app. The deck should look like the product.

---

## Slide 1 — The Hook

**Headline:** "Your regulars deserve better than a paper punch card."

**Visual:** Side-by-side — a crumpled, coffee-stained punch card on the left. The HopTrack stamp card UI on the right.

**No bullets. Just the image and the headline.**

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem (Feel the Pain)

**Headline:** "You're running blind."

**3 punchy stats (use real ones when we have them, proxies until then):**
- 67% of taproom owners have no data on who their regulars are
- The average paper punch card is redeemed less than 30% of the time
- Brewery owners spend 45+ minutes/week manually updating tap lists across platforms

**Sub-copy:** "You know the beer. You know the craft. But you don't know your customer — and that's leaving money on the bar."

---

## Slide 3 — The Solution

**Headline:** "HopTrack — Track Every Pour."

**Visual:** The app on a phone. Clean. Dark. Gold.

**One sentence:** "A craft beer check-in and loyalty platform built for independent breweries — and the customers who love them."

**Two sides:**
- 🍺 **Consumer app** — customers check in, earn stamps, follow friends, discover your events
- 🏭 **Brewery dashboard** — you manage your tap list, loyalty, analytics, and events from one place

---

## Slide 4 — The Consumer App (Social Proof Engine)

**Headline:** "Every check-in is a free advertisement."

**Visual:** HomeFeed screenshot — a session card with a beer rating, a friend's reaction, a brewery name that's clickable.

**Key points:**
- Customers check in at your brewery → their friends see it → friends discover you
- XP, achievements, and streaks keep customers coming back
- Beer passport, wishlists, and ratings build an identity around your tap list

**The ask to the owner:** "When's the last time a customer told 10 of their friends about your brewery without you paying for it?"

---

## Slide 5 — Loyalty That Works

**Headline:** "Replace the punch card. Keep the regulars."

**Visual:** The stamp card UI + the loyalty dashboard showing "9/10 — Ready!" progress bars.

**Key points:**
- Digital stamp cards — no lost cards, no fraud, no guessing
- You see exactly who's close to a reward and when they last visited
- You control the program: stamps required, reward, active/paused

**The line:** "Paper punch cards disappear. HopTrack stamps don't."

---

## Slide 6 — The Dashboard

**Headline:** "Run your taproom from your phone."

**Visual:** Dashboard overview screenshot — tap list, analytics charts, events tab.

**Features to call out:**
- Drag-to-reorder tap list, 86'd toggle with one tap
- Upcoming events posted in 30 seconds
- Analytics: peak visit hours, top beers by rating, repeat visitor %
- "The Board" — your full tap list on any TV, live and auto-updating

**The line:** "Everything you used to do across four tools, in one place."

---

## Slide 7 — The Board (The Demo Closer)

**Headline:** "Your tap list. On any TV. Always live."

**Visual:** Full-screen "The Board" screenshot — dark background, gold beer names, auto-scrolling.

**One paragraph:** "No more updating the chalkboard when a keg kicks at 8pm on a Friday. HopTrack updates your TV display the moment you change your tap list — from anywhere."

**This slide usually closes the room. Let it breathe.**

## Slide 9 — Why Now

**3 bullets:**
