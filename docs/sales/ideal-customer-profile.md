## Who We Sell To First

The first 10 breweries need to be **easy wins** — operators who already feel the pain, are open to tech, and will generate the word-of-mouth that carries us to the next 100.

---

## The Sweet Spot

### Size
- **1–3 locations** (single-location preferred for v1)
- **500–3,000 monthly visitors**
- **8–25 beers on tap**
- **2–10 staff**

### Profile
- **Owner-operated taproom or brewpub** — the person who answers your email is also the one pouring pints on Saturday
- **5 years old or younger** — newer operations are more open to new tools; they don't have 20 years of "this is how we've always done it"
- **Active on Instagram** — if they care about their social presence, they care about how customers experience and share the brand. That's us.
- **Currently using paper punch cards** — this is the clearest possible signal that they feel the pain
- **No existing digital loyalty solution** — if they're on Loyaltylion or Square Loyalty, deprioritize

### Geography (Phase 1)
Target cities with dense, passionate craft beer cultures and independent taproom scenes. Avoid chains and resort/tourist towns for v1.

## Green Flags (Prioritize These)

- Uses paper punch cards or nothing at all for loyalty
- Has a TV behind the bar showing something other than sports (→ The Board pitch)
- Posts tap list updates to Instagram manually (→ tap list management pitch)
- Has a "Regulars" culture — staff knows customers by name
- Has hosted a tap takeover or release party in the last 12 months (→ events feature pitch)
- Mentioned wanting analytics but doesn't know where to start
- Drew knows them personally

## Red Flags (Deprioritize)

- Part of a chain or franchise (long sales cycles, procurement hell)
- Already on a competing loyalty platform
- Primarily a distribution brewery with no taproom
- Owner is not the day-to-day operator (hard to get buy-in)
- Fewer than 200 monthly visitors (not enough volume to show ROI)

## The Pain Hierarchy

When you talk to a brewery owner, these are the pains ranked by how much they actually hurt:

1. **"I have no idea who my regulars are"** — They know faces but not names, not visit counts, not what they drink. Zero data.
2. **"Paper punch cards get lost, don't get used, and cost me money with no visibility"** — The card lives in a drawer. They have no idea if it's working.
3. **"Updating the tap list is a pain"** — Instagram post, chalkboard update, maybe a website edit. Three places. Every time a keg kicks.
4. **"I don't know my peak hours or best-selling beers by data — just gut feel"** — They know Friday nights are busy. They don't know *how* busy vs. last month.
5. **"I wish customers could find my events more easily"** — Word of mouth and Instagram. Nothing more.

HopTrack solves all five. Lead with #1 and #2. Close with the dashboard.
