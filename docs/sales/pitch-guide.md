# HopTrack — Pitch Conversation Guide
**Owner:** Taylor · **Updated:** 2026-03-27

---

This is not a script. Brewery owners can smell a script. This is a conversation guide — the right questions to ask, the right moments to listen, and the objections you'll hear and how to handle them.

---

## The Golden Rule

**Talk about their brewery first. Talk about HopTrack second.**

Every good sales conversation starts with curiosity. Ask about their regulars. Ask about their punch card. Ask about their busiest night. By the time you're explaining HopTrack, they should already be nodding because they recognize their own problem.

---

## The Conversation Arc

### 1. Warm Open (2–3 min)
Don't pitch. Establish rapport.

- "How long have you been open?"
- "How's the season been?"
- "What's your busiest night looking like lately?"

Listen. Remember details. Use them later.

---

### 2. The Discovery Questions (5–7 min)

These are the questions that surface the pain. Ask one or two — not all of them. Let them talk.

**On loyalty:**
- "Do you have any kind of loyalty or rewards program right now?"
- "How's that working for you?" *(if they say punch cards: let them talk about the frustrations)*
- "Do you have any sense of how many people are actually using it?"

**On data:**
- "How do you know who your regulars are? Like, do you have any actual data on that?"
- "When a keg kicks, how do you figure out what to replace it with?"
- "Do you know what your peak hours are, or is it mostly gut feel?"

**On the tap list:**
- "How do you update your tap list right now? Instagram, website, chalkboard?"
- "How long does that take you per week?"

**On events:**
- "Do you do events — tap takeovers, trivia nights, release parties?"
- "How do you promote those? Just Instagram?"

---

### 3. The Pivot (30 seconds)

Once they've described at least one pain point, pivot naturally:

> "That's actually exactly the problem we built HopTrack to solve. Can I show you something?"

Then open the app or the deck. Start with whatever pain point they mentioned most.

---

### 4. The Demo Flow

Match the demo to their pain. Don't show everything — show the three things that matter to *this* person.

**If they mentioned punch cards → start with Loyalty**
Show the stamp card UI. Show the progress bar dashboard. Ask: "Imagine knowing exactly who's at 9 out of 10 stamps right now."

**If they mentioned tap list hassle → start with Tap List**
Show drag reorder. Show the 86'd toggle. Ask: "What if you could update that from your phone in two seconds, and it updated everywhere automatically — including a TV behind your bar?"

**If they mentioned events → start with Events**
Show the Events admin. 30 seconds to create a tap takeover. Ask: "And your customers see this on the app the moment you post it."

**Always end with The Board.**
It's the visual closer. Full-screen, dark, gold text, live tap list. Every single brewery owner responds to it. Let them sit with it for a moment before you say anything.

---

### 5. The Close Attempt (1 min)

Don't ask "what do you think?" Ask for a next step.

> "We're doing 14-day free trials right now — no credit card, no setup fee. I can have your tap list imported and your loyalty QR code ready for this weekend. Want to try it?"

If yes → walk them through signup right there.
If not yet → see objections below.

## Common Objections

### "We already use Untappd."
**Handle:** "Untappd is great for check-ins and beer discovery — we actually play nicely with it. But Untappd doesn't give you a loyalty program, a live TV display, or analytics on *your* customers. It's their platform. HopTrack is yours."

### "I don't have time to set something like this up."
**Handle:** "Setup takes about 30 minutes. We import your tap list, you get a QR code for your loyalty program, and your staff doesn't have to change anything. After that it runs itself. The analytics update automatically, the stamp cards are managed by the customer. You just watch the dashboard."

### "Our customers aren't really app people."
**Handle:** "Interesting — what's your average customer age? *(listen)* In our experience, the customers who *aren't* app people are exactly the ones who become your biggest regulars. They download it once because they like your brewery, and then they're checking in every visit. The app is for them. The dashboard is for you."

### "I'd need to talk to my partner / co-owner."
**Handle:** "Of course. Is there a time this week I could jump on a call with both of you — even 15 minutes? I can walk through it again and answer any questions. The free trial means there's no commitment — just a chance to see if it fits."

### "What does it cost after the trial?"
**Handle:** Walk them to the pricing slide. Lead with Tap ($49). Mention Cask ($149) only if they've shown interest in loyalty and analytics. "Less than the cost of one keg per month."

### "What happens to our data?"
**Handle:** "Your data is yours. We use Supabase with row-level security — which means your customer data is isolated to your account. We don't sell data, we don't share it with other breweries, and you can export it anytime. Privacy policy is at hoptrack.beer/privacy."

### "We tried something like this before and it didn't stick."
**Handle:** "What happened with that one?" *(listen)* "That's a common story — a lot of these tools are built for restaurants or retail and adapted for breweries as an afterthought. HopTrack was built for breweries from day one. Drew Callahan, who runs [his taproom] in Asheville, has been advising us since the start. The 86'd toggle, the tap reorder, The Board — none of that exists in generic loyalty tools."

## What Not to Do

- **Don't lead with pricing.** Let them fall in love first.
- **Don't show everything.** Three features, well, beats ten features rushed.
- **Don't apologize for the product.** It's good. Own it.
- **Don't pressure.** Brewery owners talk to each other constantly. A bad experience gets around.
- **Don't pitch until you've asked at least two questions.** Ever.

## After the Conversation

Whether they said yes or not yet:

1. **Send a follow-up email within 2 hours** with:
   - A one-line summary of the pain they mentioned
   - A link to start the free trial: `hoptrack.beer/for-breweries`
   - One screenshot or feature that matched their specific pain
   - Your direct line

2. **Log the contact** in the target brewery tracker (`target-breweries.md`)

3. **If they said yes:** schedule the 30-minute onboarding call immediately

4. **If they said "maybe in a few months":** set a reminder for 6 weeks out and check in with a product update that's relevant to what they cared about
