04 — The Mark

One line.
One loop.
One hop.

The mark is a single continuous stroke. It enters at the crown, traces the outer hop silhouette wide to the right — establishing the full scale of the form — then loops back across and spirals inward with one confident, unhurried turn before settling near the center. The stem drops clean below.

The line never crosses itself. It never doubles back. It knows exactly where it's going. That restraint — the decision to stop when it has said enough — is what makes it work as a mark rather than as an illustration.

It is a hop. It is a tracking path. It is the signature of someone who understood both.

08 — Usage Guide
Clear space

Give it room

Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the "H" in the wordmark on all four sides. The mark breathes — don't crowd it.

Minimum size

Sizes

Full lockup: 140px wide / 35mm print minimum. Mark alone: 24px minimum digital, 8mm print. Below 24px use a filled silhouette export.

Backgrounds

Approved surfaces

Full color on #1A1A1A and darker. Dark-on-cream on #F5F0E8 and #EDE7D9. Never on photographic backgrounds without a solid band.

Stroke weight

Don't touch the line

The stroke weight is part of the mark. Never reduce or increase it independently. Scale the mark as a whole — never resize the path alone.

Color

Approved colors only

Gold (#C8962E) on dark. Gold Dark (#A67820) on cream. White (#F5F0E8) monochrome on dark. Near-black (#1A1A1A) on cream. No gradients, no drop shadows.

File formats

Export formats

SVG for all digital use — it's a single path, transfers perfectly. PNG@3× for app contexts and email. Never scale a 1× PNG up. Never rasterize below 128px.
