The Art of Cocktail Glassware
Glasses
12
A bartender's glassware is their toolkit. Each shape controls dilution, temperature, presentation, and how the drink meets your lips. The wrong glass won't ruin the cocktail — but the right one elevates it.
5–7 oz
Coupe
Champagne Coupe · Cocktail Coupe
The modern bartender's darling — it's replaced the martini glass in most craft bars. The wide, shallow bowl is forgiving to carry, beautiful to garnish, and its rounded shape softens every sip. Vintage glamour meets daily practicality.
Best Served With
Daiquiri
Sidecar
Manhattan
Gimlet
Aviation
Bee's Knees
5–8 oz
Martini Glass
Cocktail Glass · V-Glass
The icon. That V-shape isn't just style — the wide surface area releases aromatics, and the angled sides keep the olive from rolling. Notoriously easy to spill, which is why coupes have taken over, but nothing else looks quite this cinematic.
Best Served With
Martini
Cosmopolitan
Vesper
Gibson
Espresso Martini
6–10 oz
Rocks Glass
Old Fashioned · Lowball · Tumbler
Heavy, wide, and built for a large ice cube. The thick base gives satisfying heft, the wide mouth lets you nose the whiskey, and it fits a large format ice sphere or cube that melts slowly to keep dilution in check.
Best Served With
Old Fashioned
Negroni
Whiskey Sour
Sazerac
Spirits Neat
10–14 oz
Highball
Tall Glass · Collins
The tall glass for long drinks. A generous pour of mixer over spirit and ice — the height keeps the drink cold, shows off the color, and the straight sides hold carbonation longer than a wide-mouth would. The backbone of casual cocktail service.
Best Served With
Highball
Tom Collins
Gin & Tonic
Mojito
Dark & Stormy
Paloma
5–6 oz
Nick & Nora
The Thin Man Glass
Named after the cocktail-loving couple from The Thin Man films. A more elegant, spill-resistant alternative to both the martini and coupe — the slightly inward-curving rim holds aromatics in while allowing graceful sipping.
Best Served With
Martini
Manhattan
Bijou
Last Word
Stirred Cocktails
12–16 oz
Copper Mug
Moscow Mule Mug · Hammered Mug
Copper conducts cold instantly — pick one up and your hand knows this drink means business. The metal chills the cocktail fast and keeps it frigid, while the hammered surface catches candlelight. Inseparable from the Moscow Mule experience.
Best Served With
Moscow Mule
Kentucky Mule
Mexican Mule
London Mule
15–20 oz
Hurricane
Poco Grande · Tropical Glass
Born on Bourbon Street — the curvy, lamp-shaped glass that screams tropical vacation. The wide bowl accommodates crushed ice, fruit garnishes, and generous pours. It's a party vessel, not a tasting one, and that's exactly the point.
Best Served With
Hurricane
Piña Colada
Blue Lagoon
Singapore Sling
Frozen Drinks
10–14 oz
Margarita Glass
Welled Coupe · Double-Bowl
The wide, stepped rim exists for one reason: salt. That broad lip gives you a generous runway of salt (or tajín) that meets your tongue with every sip. The double-bowl design is iconic but impractical — many bars use rocks glasses instead.
Best Served With
Margarita
Frozen Margarita
Daiquiri
Frozen Cocktails
10–12 oz
Julep Cup
Mint Julep Cup · Silver Cup
Traditionally pewter or silver-plated — the metal frosts on the outside when filled with crushed ice, creating a beautiful, tactile drinking experience. The cup is as much a part of the cocktail's ritual as the bourbon and mint inside it.
Best Served With
Mint Julep
Whiskey Smash
Cobbler
Crushed Ice Drinks
6 oz
Glencairn
Whisky Glass · Nosing Glass
The official whisky tasting glass — developed in Scotland with master blenders. The wide bowl allows swirling, the tapered mouth concentrates aromas, and the thick solid base anchors the glass comfortably in your hand. No stem needed.
Best Served With
Scotch Whisky
Bourbon
Rye Whiskey
Irish Whiskey
Spirits Neat
12–24 oz
Tiki Mug
Ceramic Tiki · Idol Mug
The vessel is the experience. Carved ceramic faces, totems, and skulls — each tiki bar has their signature mug. The thick ceramic insulates frozen drinks, and the playful shapes are collectible pieces of pop-Polynesian bar culture.
Best Served With
Mai Tai
Zombie
Scorpion Bowl
Navy Grog
Tiki Drinks
1–2 oz
Shot Glass
Shooter · Jigger
Small, heavy-bottomed, and designed for a single purpose — one fast pour, one fast drink. The thick base survives getting slammed on the bar. Also doubles as a measuring jigger in home bars. Simple, universal, indestructible.
Best Served With
Tequila
Whiskey
Vodka
Shooters
Amaro
